About Me

I am an inspired and passionate graduate student currently pursuing a Master's degree in TESOL at New Uzbekistan University. I enjoy teaching language and my goal is to ensure that I provide an interesting and positive learning experience that encourages students to participate in the learning process.

My areas of academic interest are the effective teaching methodologies in the English language, especially in the English as a Foreign Language (EFL) context.

Of particular interest to me is the process of developing the listening and writing skills of students using the learner-centered and task-based method. I also appreciate the combination of soft skills with language teaching, as it is crucial to students' academic performance and future careers.

My Beliefs

Teaching Philosophy

As a teacher, I am convinced that each student can grow, develop, and be successful under the condition of appropriate support, guidance, and a nurturing environment. Being a young and early-career teacher, I structure my lessons in a manner that is interactive, student-centered, and engaging ‚Äî encouraging learners to ask questions, share ideas, take risks, and creatively and critically apply their knowledge.

I attempt to make the classroom environment a friendly, supportive, and inclusive space in which students feel free to express themselves, experiment without the fear of error, and learn to think independently. My instructions focus on the development of core language skills ‚Äî speaking, listening, reading, and writing ‚Äî alongside soft skills including communication, teamwork, critical thinking, adaptability, and creativity.

I engage in problem-based learning, project work, collaborative learning, task-based teaching, and experiential learning to ensure that every lesson is engaging, meaningful, and active. Ultimately, I want to develop students into lifelong learners who are self-confident, imaginative, responsible, and socially conscious.
